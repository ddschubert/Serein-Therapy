Serein Therapy exists to support teens and adults as they navigate identity shifts, life transitions, and periods of growth. Grounded in a person–centred and culturally responsive lens, we offer a thoughtful, collaborative space where clients feel seen, respected, and understood. Our work supports people in making meaning of change, strengthening self–trust, and moving forward in ways that align with who they are and who they are becoming.

Therapeutic Approaches

CBT helps you identify and change unhelpful thoughts and behaviors, so you can feel more in control of your mood and reactions.

DBT teaches you skills to manage intense emotions and relationships, helping you respond to stress and conflict more effectively.

Narrative therapy allows you to explore your story, separate yourself from problems, and rewrite your experiences in a more empowering way.

A little about me

I’m Doré, founder of Serein Therapy, a virtual practice dedicated to supporting people navigating complex life transitions. I hold my Canadian Certified Counsellor (CCC) designation and graduated from Yorkville University with a Master's of Arts in Counseling Psychology (MACP). I built this practice to create a space where therapy feels safe, validating, and practical because you deserve more than generic advice or “one-size-fits-all” solutions.

My approach is flexible and person-centered, meaning I meet you where you are, adapt to your goals, and give you tools that actually help you navigate pain, stress, or life changes. I focus on both mindset and practical strategies so therapy feels empowering, not exhausting.

I specialize in supporting people with chronic illness, intellectual disability, or major life transitions, including those adjusting to new diagnoses or living with long-term pain. If you’re looking for a therapist who understands what it’s like to manage life and health simultaneously, who validates your experience, and helps you reclaim control and confidence, you’re in the right place.

Land Acknowledgment

At Serein Therapy, we acknowledge that we live and work on the traditional territories of the Siksikaitsitapi (Blackfoot Confederacy) including the Siksika, Kainai, and Piikani Nations as well as the Tsuut’ina Nation and the Stoney Nakoda Nations (Bearspaw, Chiniki, and Wesley). We honour the enduring relationship these Nations have with this land and give thanks for the opportunity to live, work, and learn here.
